Overview

Case study for the Responsive Web app of SOMA (translates to body). A healthcare concept app I designed during my study at CareerFoundry.

Problem statement

People need a reliable and affordable way to find solutions for their health conditions wherever they are because the process of getting diagnosed or researching for answers can be expensive, time-consuming and maybe even unreliable.

User-centered design process

Discover

Competitor analysis

Ada ( 10.000.000 downloads ) and avey ( 500.000 downloads ) were my main focus for the research based on their user base and features. Started by getting to know the key competitors in the market, to get an idea of users' expectations and painpoint opportunities to meet their unfulfilled needs. By doing competitor analysis and market research, I was able to identify these quickly.

Research design

I was prepared by creating an interview script and over 14 questions to acquire my qualitative data. At the same time I published my surveys receiving quantitative data.

#Decisionstaken

Throughout the whole design process, my focus was to improve the usablity and experience of people using the app.

Introduction

Moved “Log in” and “Sign up” buttons lower. Made it clear what’s clickable and moved everything in the thumb zone of the user.

Homescreen/Dashboard

Visual hierarchy and reduction of cognitive load for the user was the focus in these screens. You can see the changes are significant.

Diagnosis screen

Visual hierarchy and unity elevate the experience of this screen. After numerous iterations, I designed the information presented in a logical way for people using the app.

I iterated and tested my design decisions until I was getting consistent results.

Design

Design language system

The voice of SOMA needs to be personal to give the feeling of a conversation with an actual person, but also in a professional level since it’s a health related product. Try not to use complicated health jargon but be very specific with the names of diverse conditions.

Solution

SOMA has focused on establishing features that will allow users to get diagnosed efficiently by an A.I. driven bot while it asks specific questions depending on the symptoms users have inputted. After the “questionnaire” users will receive a diagnosis, allowing them to get answers and possible next steps to any of their conditions. The option to follow up with a real doctor via video call, for a more intensive diagnosis, will be available for a fee, making the experience more reliable and stress-free to users.

New hypothesis

On a higher-level, I would like to start looking on the doctor’s perspective as well. One of my future goals for the app is to create another hypothesis and build the interface doctors will use while using SOMA. It’s an important aspect of the UX design process to look not only the “typical” user’s side but also the professional’s side.
